Pollutants

Particulate pollution is pollution of an environment that consists of particles suspended in some medium. There are three primary forms: atmospheric particulate matter, marine debris, and space debris. Some particles are released directly from a specific source, while others form in chemical reactions in the atmosphere.
  1. Particulate Matter
  2. Nitrogen Dioxide
  3. Ozone
  4. Carbon Monoxide
  5. Sulfur Dioxide

Effects

Air pollution can have many harmful effects on both humans and the environment. It can cause diseases, allergies, and even death to humans; it can also cause harm to other living organisms such as animals and food crops, and may damage the natural environment (for example, climate change, ozone depletion or habitat degradation) or built environment (for example, acid rain).
  1. Air pollution can cause diseases.
  2. It can also cause harm to living organisms and food crops.
  3. They also include discomfort such as irritation to the nose, throat, eyes, or skin.
  4. Air pollution can also cause headaches, dizziness, and nausea.
  5. It can result in Asthma Attack also.

Preventions

We have seen the different causes of air pollution through human activities. Their adverse effects on the environment and health are also discussed. Now, it is time to see how we can prevent air pollution by taking control measures of air pollution. Air pollution can be reduced only if there is a collective effort from everyone’s side.
  1. Use of Public Transport
  2. Reduce the Consumption of Electricity
  3. Recycling and Reuse of Products
  4. Avoid Plastic bags
  5. Avoid burning of Plastics

(i) The primary causes of outdoor air pollution are solid, liquid particles called aerosols & gase from vehicles emissions, construction activities, factories, burning stubble & fossil fuels and wildfire, etc.
(ii) Main causes of indoor air pollution are harmful gases from cooking fuels (such as wood, crop wastes, charcoal, coal and dung), damp, mould smoke, chemicals from cleaning materials, etc.

Indoor air pollution in India is as dangerous as outdoor pollution, because the air pollutants come inside the houses or buildings through doors, windows and ventilation.

In India, you must use an air purifier or fresh air machine at home or office indoor and close all the doors, windows and ventilations when the outdoor air quality index (aqi) in India is very high. Proper ventilation is highly recommended only when outdoor air quality is improving and moderate AQI range.
